Abstract

Current text-to-audio models struggle with fine-grained control over the acoustic environment, as text prompts are often insufficient to describe complex physical scenes. To address this, we propose VividVoice, a unified framework for scene-aware speech synthesis driven by multimodal prompts. Our model can generate high-fidelity speech within a controllable acoustic environment, where the speech content is dictated by text, and the acoustic scene (e.g., background sounds, reverberation) is intuitively controlled by a visual image. At the core of our framework is the Decoupled Memory-based Scene-Voice Alignment (D-MSVA) module, an innovative architecture that explicitly disentangles speaker and environment characteristics from the mixed-modality inputs. Complemented by a contrastive decoupling supervision strategy, our model learns a robust, compositional representation of voice and scene. We also contribute a new, large-scale dataset specifically constructed for this task. Experiments show that VividVoice significantly outperforms existing methods in generating immersive, high-quality speech that is consistent with its multimodal context.
